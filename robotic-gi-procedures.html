Robotic Procedures

A robotic surgery procedure involves a surgeon controlling robotic arms equipped with tiny surgical instruments from a nearby console. The surgeon makes small incisions to insert the instruments and a camera, which provides a magnified 3D view of the surgical site.

A robotic surgery procedure involves a surgeon controlling robotic arms equipped with tiny surgical instruments from a nearby console. The surgeon makes small incisions to insert the instruments and a camera, which provides a magnified 3D view of the surgical site.

robot translates the surgeon's hand movements with enhanced precision and dexterity, allowing for complex and minimally invasive operations with benefits like reduced pain and faster recovery.

How it works

The patient is typically given general anesthesia to ensure they are asleep and pain-free.

The surgeon makes a few small incisions in the patient's body to access the surgical area.

Thin tubes called ports are inserted through these incisions, and the robotic arms with surgical instruments and a camera are placed through them

The surgeon sits at a console near the operating table, controlling the movements of the robotic arms and viewing a high-definition, 3D image of the surgical site.
