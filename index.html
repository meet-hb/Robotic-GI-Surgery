Why Robotic GI Surgery

Robotic Surgery represents a groundbreaking leap in medical technology, revolutionizing the approach to gastrointestinal (GI) surgeries. This innovative technique utilizes robotic systems to aid surgeons in conducting minimally invasive procedures, enhancing precision, control, and flexibility.

While the idea of robotic surgery has existed for many years, its integration into gastroenterology has created exciting new opportunities for effectively addressing complex GI Diseases, leading to improved outcomes and higher patient satisfaction.

GI Diseases

Esophagus

The esophagus is a muscular tube in the digestive system that connects the pharynx (throat) to the stomach, carrying food and liquid down via a process called peristalsis.

  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Pain with swallowing
  • Feeling of a lump in the throat
  • Regurgitation
Learn More

Stomach & Duodenum

The stomach and duodenum are key parts of the digestive system where food is broken down and nutrients are absorbed.

  • Abdominal pain
  • Bloating and gas
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Early fullness
Learn More

Small bowel

Includes conditions like Crohn's disease, which causes chronic inflammation.

  • Abdominal pain and discomfort
  • Bloating and gas
  • Changes in bowel habits
  • Nausea and vomiting
Learn More

Liver

Liver diseases are conditions that affect the liver's function and can be caused by viruses, excessive alcohol, fat buildup, autoimmune issues, or inherited conditions.

  • Jaundice
  • Fatigue
  • Swelling
  • Nausea or vomiting
Learn More

Gall bladder

Gallbladder diseases include inflammation (cholecystitis), gallstones (cholelithiasis), and functional disorders where the gallbladder doesn't move bile properly (biliary dyskinesia).

  • Pain
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Bloating and indigestion
  • Fat-soluble vitamin deficiencies
Learn More

Bile ducts

Bile duct diseases are conditions that affect the ducts carrying bile from the liver to the small intestine, causing symptoms like jaundice, itching, and abdominal pain.

  • Jaundice
  • Itching
  • Dark urine
  • Pale stools
Learn More

Pancreas

Diseases of the pancreas include pancreatitis, pancreatic cancer, cystic fibrosis, and diabetes.

  • Abdominal pain
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Fever
  • Rapid pulse
Learn More
