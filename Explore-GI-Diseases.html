Explore GI Diseases

Gastrointestinal (GI) diseases are disorders affecting the digestive system, including the esophagus, stomach, and intestines. They range from common issues like GERD and IBS to more serious conditions such as Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD), Crohn's disease, and peptic ulcers

These diseases can be classified as acute (sudden onset) or chronic (long-term), and functional (no visible physical abnormality) or structural (physical damage or abnormalities).

GI diseases can be acute or chronic:

  • An acute GI disease is one that happens suddenly and lasts a short period of time.
  • A chronic GI disease can affect you for many months or years.
  • A functional GI disease is when your healthcare provider can’t see any physical or structural issues when they examine your digestive tract.
  • A structural GI disease is when your healthcare provider can see evidence of an issue when they examine you.

Common gastrointestinal diseases

Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)

A condition where stomach acid flows back into the esophagus.

Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS)

A chronic disorder affecting the stomach and intestines, causing symptoms like pain, bloating, and changes in bowel habits.

Inflammatory bowel disease (IBD)

A group of chronic inflammatory conditions of the digestive tract, including Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis.

Celiac disease

An autoimmune disorder triggered by consuming gluten, which damages the small intestine.

Gastroenteritis

An infection and inflammation of the stomach and intestines, often called "stomach flu," caused by viruses, bacteria, or parasites.

Peptic ulcers

Open sores that develop on the lining of the stomach or the first part of the small intestine.
